About This Project

Our mission, methodology, commitment to historical accuracy, and the people behind this educational initiative.

Our Mission

This website exists for one reason: every Indian has the right to know their true history.

For decades, Indian textbooks have presented a sanitized version of the Sultanate period ‚Äî one that minimizes the documented impact of Islamic rule on Hindu civilization, culture, and heritage. Bahlul Lodi, the founder of the Lodi dynasty, is typically described in a few paragraphs as a "tolerant" and "humane" ruler, with no mention of the systemic religious discrimination he perpetuated, the military devastation his campaigns caused, or the dynasty of destruction he founded.

This project aims to present the complete, documented, source-backed historical record ‚Äî not opinions, not assumptions, but facts drawn from primary historical chronicles written by medieval historians, many of whom were sympathetic to the Sultanate itself.

Our Methodology

We follow a rigorous methodology to ensure historical accuracy:

  • Primary sources first: All claims are derived from primary historical chronicles ‚Äî Tarikh-i-Daudi, Ferishta, Tabaqat-i-Akbari, Makhzan-i-Afghani, and others.
  • Cross-reference: We cross-reference claims across multiple sources to ensure accuracy.
  • Context preservation: We present historical events in their full context, including the social, political, and religious dimensions that textbooks omit.
  • Transparent sourcing: Every significant claim is cited with its source. Readers can verify any claim by consulting the original texts.
  • No fabrication: We do not invent or embellish. The documented historical record is sufficiently significant without fabrication.

This website openly acknowledges its perspective: it presents history from the viewpoint of the Indian civilization that was impacted by Sultanate rule. This is not a neutral "both sides" presentation ‚Äî because the destruction of a civilization's temples, the taxation of its people for their religion, and the suppression of its learning does not have "another side." We present facts. The facts speak for themselves.
